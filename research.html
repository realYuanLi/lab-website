Research

Research areas

Our research spans methodology development and applied collaborations. The themes below are interrelated — most projects combine two or more.

Methods

Longitudinal methodology

We develop statistical methods for multivariate, irregular, and high-dimensional longitudinal health data. Current directions include functional data analysis for sparsely sampled trajectories, joint models for biomarkers and time-to-event outcomes, and dynamic prediction frameworks that update risk as new measurements arrive.

Pediatrics

Risk prediction in pediatric disease

In collaboration with the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia, we build prediction models for outcomes in congenital heart disease and other pediatric conditions, integrating evidence from registries, hospital encounters, and administrative claims. The lab also develops methods to combine data sources with different sampling and measurement properties.

Safety

Pharmacovigilance

We work on hierarchical and Bayesian signal-detection methods for federal spontaneous-reporting systems, including the FDA's FAERS and CDC's VAERS. The aim is to improve the precision of safety signals while controlling false-discovery rates at the scale these systems require.

Infrastructure

Distributed research networks

We design privacy-preserving statistical methods for distributed research networks — most notably PEDSnet — including distributed regression, federated maximum-likelihood estimation, and meta-analytic infrastructure that allows institutions to contribute to shared analyses without sharing patient-level data.

mHealth

Mobile health and dynamic intervention

Patient-generated streams from wearables and ecological momentary assessment offer dense, real-time signal but pose new statistical challenges. We work on adaptive decision rules and just-in-time adaptive intervention frameworks estimated from these streams.

Public Health

Epidemic modelling

In partnership with CHOP's PolicyLab, we contributed county-level transmission modelling for SARS-CoV-2 during the COVID-19 pandemic, used to inform federal guidance. The methods generalise to other emerging infectious diseases with heterogeneous local dynamics.

Funding
