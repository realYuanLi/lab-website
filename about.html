About the lab

The Huang Lab develops statistical methodology to understand the dynamics of disease activity and to inform health management using multivariate longitudinal data.

Portrait

Jing Huang, PhD

Principal Investigator · Associate Professor of Biostatistics

Dr. Huang is Associate Professor of Biostatistics in the Department of Biostatistics, Epidemiology and Informatics at the Perelman School of Medicine, University of Pennsylvania. She is a faculty member of the Center for Clinical Epidemiology and Biostatistics (CCEB) and a senior scholar affiliated with PolicyLab at the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia.

Her research focuses on statistical methods for multivariate longitudinal health data, with applications to risk prediction, disease-trajectory characterisation, and data integration across registries, hospitals, and claims systems. She is the Principal Investigator of an NIH R01 on disease-trajectory characterisation and a Multiple Principal Investigator on a PCORI-funded patient-centered outcomes research project. She led the first U.S. county-level COVID-19 transmission modelling project, which informed guidance from the White House Coronavirus Task Force.

Contact: jing14@pennmedicine.upenn.edu

Lab focus

The lab works at the interface of biostatistics, clinical epidemiology, and health informatics. We are most interested in problems where the data are longitudinal, multivariate, and fragmented — registries, EHRs, claims, and patient-generated streams — and where careful statistical modelling can recover meaningful clinical patterns.
